🌊 Engineering Resilience for Africa's Largest Floating Solar Project

This project delivers a high-resolution wave climate assessment for the proposed 1.2GW floating solar installation at Chirahara, Lake Kariba. Combining state-of-the-art atmospheric modeling with advanced wave simulation to ensure structural integrity and optimal design.

Timeline: 6 months Location: Chirahara, Lake Kariba Capacity: 1.2 GW

Key Challenge

Design wave heights for floating solar infrastructure in Africa's largest man-made lake with complex wind patterns.

Three-Stage Analysis Methodology

Our approach combines global reanalysis with high-resolution local modeling:

1
📡

ERA5 Reanalysis

30-year historical weather data (1990-2020) at 31km resolution from ECMWF

Output: Initial wave climate assessment
2
🌀

WRF Downscaling

Weather Research & Forecasting model at 1km resolution for Lake Kariba

Output: Site-specific wind fields
3
🌊

SWAN Wave Modeling

Simulating Waves Nearshore model with detailed bathymetry

Output: Design wave parameters
4
📊

Validation & Analysis

Ground station comparison and extreme value statistics

Output: Bankable design criteria

Key Findings & Design Parameters

Critical wave statistics for engineering design at Chirahara site

Significant Wave Height

0.85m

100-year return period maximum wave height

Seasonal range: 0.25m (summer) - 0.85m (winter)

Dominant Wave Direction

SE

Southeast (135°) prevailing wave direction

Max fetch: 280km along SE-NW axis

Peak Wave Period

2.8s

Average peak period during storm conditions

Range: 1.5s - 4.2s (95% confidence)

Engineering Recommendations

Mooring Design

Design for 1.2m maximum wave height with 2.0 safety factor

Operations Window

Optimal maintenance: May-September (low wave conditions)

Load Factors

Dynamic amplification factor: 1.3 for wave loading
